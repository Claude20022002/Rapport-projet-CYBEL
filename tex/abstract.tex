\section*{\centering ABSTRACT}
\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{ABSTRACT}

Mobile service robots are typically delivered with a \textbf{closed} software
ecosystem: proprietary application, undocumented communication protocol, and
no public API. This situation prevents end users from customising the robot's
behaviour to suit their specific needs. The \textbf{CYBEL} project, carried
out as part of a final-year internship (PFA), aims to design an
\textbf{independent command and interaction platform} for a mobile reception
robot \textit{CIOT TY1251D-03195}.

The approach relies on an incremental, non-destructive \textbf{reverse
engineering} of the robot's internal communication protocol: scanning of
exposed network services, introspection of \textbf{ROS} topics and services
via \textit{rosbridge/rosapi}, and systematic verification of each command's
effectiveness. On this basis, a \textbf{three-layer architecture} was
designed: a \textbf{Python SDK} offering interchangeable simulated
(\textit{mock}) and real implementations, a \textbf{FastAPI} backend
(REST + WebSocket), and a \textbf{Vite/TypeScript} web interface.

At this stage, connectivity with the robot has been established, and the
\textbf{telemetry}, \textbf{velocity control}, and \textbf{autonomous
navigation} protocols have been reconstructed and integrated. The main
remaining challenge concerns the \textbf{text-to-speech (TTS)} channel,
which is not exposed through ROS and requires additional access to the
Android subsystem.

\vspace{0.5cm}
\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} service robotics, protocol reverse engineering,
ROS, rosbridge, FastAPI, human-robot interaction, autonomous navigation,
text-to-speech.
